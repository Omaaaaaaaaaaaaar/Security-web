Instant Cameras Description: Cameras that print photos instantly, often using special self-developing film (e.g., Polaroid). Best For: Casual photographers and those who enjoy instant gratification with physical photos. Key Features: Instant photo printing Compact and fun to use Often used for casual or creative photo-taking 8. 360 Cameras Description: These cameras capture a full 360-degree image or video, offering an immersive experience. Best For: People creating virtual reality (VR) content or anyone who wants to capture a complete environment. Key Features: Captures a full 360-degree field of view Often used for VR or immersive video experiences Typically compact and easy to use

Cam 2

Bridge Cameras Description: These are mid-range cameras that combine features of both compact cameras and DSLRs. They typically have a large zoom range but do not offer interchangeable lenses. Best For: Those who want a versatile zoom lens without the complexity or cost of a DSLR. Key Features: Fixed lens with a wide zoom range Optical or electronic viewfinder Larger body compared to compact cameras Manual settings available in many models 6. Film Cameras Description: Traditional cameras that use photographic film to capture images. These can range from simple point-and-shoot models to advanced manual cameras. Best For: Photography enthusiasts who enjoy the process of shooting with film and the unique aesthetic it provides. Key Features: Uses 35mm, medium format, or large format film Manual or automatic exposure controls Requires film rolls and developing

Cam 3

Compact Cameras (Point-and-Shoot) Description: These are small, easy-to-use cameras with fixed lenses, often with automatic settings. Best For: Casual photographers, travelers, or anyone who wants a portable camera without the complexity of manual controls. Key Features: Fixed lens Simple to use (mostly automatic settings) Compact size Built-in flash 4. Action Cameras Description: Small, rugged cameras designed to be mounted on equipment or used in extreme conditions. Best For: Outdoor adventurers, sports enthusiasts, and anyone filming in challenging environments. Key Features: Waterproof, shockproof, and durable Wide-angle lenses Compact and mountable Often designed for 4K or high-resolution video recording

Cam 4

1. DSLR Cameras (Digital Single-Lens Reflex) Description: These cameras use a mirror mechanism to reflect light from the lens to an optical viewfinder. Best For: Professionals and enthusiasts who need high image quality, fast autofocus, and the ability to change lenses. Key Features: Interchangeable lenses Optical viewfinder Large sensors (for better image quality) Manual controls 2. Mirrorless Cameras Description: Similar to DSLRs but without the mirror mechanism. They offer a more compact design. Best For: Those who want a high-quality camera with interchangeable lenses but prefer a lighter, more portable option. Key Features: Interchangeable lenses Electronic viewfinder or LCD screen for composing shots Faster autofocus in some models Compact and lightweight
